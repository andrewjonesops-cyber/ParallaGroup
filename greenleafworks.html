ChangeWorks™ Program

"Cannabis? Are you serious?"
Yes. And here's why.

We get the reaction. Cannabis carries stigma that has nothing to do with the science — and everything to do with decades of narrative. GreenLeafWorks™ exists to change that conversation: for patients who deserve honest answers, and for dispensaries who deserve to run a real business.

Two Sides of One Mission

For Patients

Stigma is not a clinical argument. We help patients and their families cut through the noise — what the data supports, where evidence is still developing, and how to have productive conversations with their care teams about medical cannabis as part of a broader treatment approach.

For Dispensaries

A legal cannabis business shouldn't have to operate like it's underground. We help dispensaries build professional systems — operational, financial, and compliance-ready — so they can run a real business in an environment that still creates unnecessary barriers.

The Patient Side — What the Evidence Shows

Medical cannabis is not a cure-all — but in specific conditions, the clinical evidence is real, peer-reviewed, and significant. Here is where the data is strongest.

39%
Seizure frequency reduction
CBD reduced convulsive seizures by 39% in Dravet syndrome patients in a landmark NEJM-published randomized controlled trial. [1]
53%
Patients with ≥50% fewer seizures
In LGS/Dravet expanded access program, 53% of patients saw major motor seizures cut by half or more at 12 weeks. [2]
85%
Caregivers reported improved seizure frequency
In the BECOME caregiver survey of Epidiolex users, 85% reported improvement in overall seizure frequency. [3]
47
U.S. states permit some form of medical cannabis
As of 2024, 47 states, D.C., and 3 territories allow some form of medical cannabis use. [4]
Epilepsy & Seizure Disorders

The FDA-approved cannabidiol drug Epidiolex has demonstrated significant, peer-reviewed seizure reduction in Lennox-Gastaut syndrome, Dravet syndrome, and tuberous sclerosis complex — conditions where multiple prior medications had failed.

Source: Devinsky et al., NEJM 2017; Thiele et al., Lancet 2018; FDA approval 2018 [1][2]
Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea & Vomiting

FDA-approved synthetic cannabinoid medications (dronabinol, nabilone) are established treatments for chemotherapy-related nausea and appetite loss in cancer and HIV patients. These represent the clearest regulatory endorsements of cannabis-derived medicine.

Source: FDA approvals; Mayo Clinic [5]
Chronic Pain & Multiple Sclerosis Spasticity

Research supports cannabis-based treatments for spasticity in MS patients. Chronic pain is the most commonly cited reason for medical cannabis use, and while clinical guidelines remain cautious, evidence for certain pain types — particularly nerve-related — shows promise.

Source: University of Sydney review, 2025; CDC [4][6]
Anxiety & Depression

52% of medical cannabis users report using it for anxiety, and 40% for depression. Results in clinical research are mixed — some oral CBD formulations have shown benefit, while other studies show no difference over placebo. This is an area where honest, nuanced guidance matters most.

Source: International Cannabis Policy Study, PMC 2022 [7]
Crohn's Disease & IBS

Cannabis use for gastrointestinal conditions including Crohn's disease and IBS has been widely reported by patients. Current clinical evidence is still developing, with some studies showing symptom relief and others finding inconclusive results. Research is ongoing.

Source: Frontiers in Pharmacology 2022; O'Dell et al. 2025 [8]
PTSD

17% of medical cannabis users report using it for PTSD. The evidence base is growing, particularly for veterans, though results vary significantly by product type and patient profile. This is recognized as a qualifying condition in most medical cannabis states.

Source: International Cannabis Policy Study, PMC 2022 [7]
A note on evidence: GreenLeafWorks™ is committed to presenting the evidence honestly. A comprehensive JAMA review published in December 2025 — analyzing over 2,500 studies — found that while evidence is strongest for seizure disorders and chemotherapy-related nausea, evidence for many other conditions remains limited or mixed. We believe informed patients make better decisions, and that means presenting both what the data supports and where uncertainty remains. [9]
The Dispensary Side — A Legal Business Treated as a Liability

Dispensaries operate legally under state law — yet they face structural barriers that no other legal business in the United States encounters at scale.

~40
Financial institutions with dedicated cannabis banking programs
Out of thousands of U.S. banks and credit unions, only an estimated 40 have active, dedicated cannabis banking programs. [10]
27.3%
Cannabis businesses that are profitable
Compared to 65.3% of all U.S. small businesses, only 27.3% of cannabis businesses were profitable in 2024. [11]
280E
Federal tax code blocking standard deductions
IRC 280E prevents cannabis businesses from deducting ordinary expenses, creating an effective tax rate far above comparable industries. [10]
$115B
U.S. economic contribution in 2024
Despite banking and regulatory barriers, cannabis contributed $115.2 billion to the U.S. economy in 2024, supporting over 440,000 jobs. [12]
Banking & Cash Operations

Most major banks refuse cannabis clients due to federal scheduling. This forces dispensaries into cash-heavy operations — creating security risks, payroll complications, and compliance headaches that drain resources and limit growth. The SAFE Banking Act has passed the House multiple times but remains stalled in the Senate as of 2025.

Payment Processing

Major credit card networks do not permit cannabis-related transactions. Most dispensaries cannot accept Visa or Mastercard, pushing customers toward cash or workaround solutions that carry their own compliance risks and customer friction.

Tax Burden Under 280E

Under federal tax code 280E, cannabis businesses cannot deduct standard business expenses. This creates effective tax rates dramatically higher than comparable industries — a direct financial penalty on state-legal businesses with no equivalent in any other sector.

Insurance & Professional Services

Beyond banking, many professional services providers — accountants, insurers, landlords, lawyers — remain hesitant to engage with cannabis businesses. The result is a higher cost of doing business across virtually every operational function.
