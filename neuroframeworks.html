ChangeWorks™ Program

People deserve environments built around how they actually work — not the other way around.

NeuroFrameWorks™ is Paralla Group's neurodivergent-centered accessibility program, focused on making everyday environments and workflows more accessible for ND people. It helps teams, schools, and organizations redesign processes, communication, and spaces so people do not need to mask constantly, overcompensate, or burn out just to keep up.

What NeuroFrameWorks™ Does

A non-clinical, accessibility-focused program — supporting better lives through better structures, not diagnosis or treatment.

Executive-Function–Aware Workflows

Redesigning task structures, documentation, and processes to work with executive function realities — reducing the cognitive overhead of getting work done for ND people without adding burden to everyone else.

Cognitive Load Reduction

Identifying and removing unnecessary friction in digital and physical systems — unclear instructions, excessive context-switching, ambiguous expectations — so people can focus energy on the actual work.

Sensory Environment Planning

Assessing and improving physical and digital spaces to reduce sensory overload — lighting, noise, visual clutter, notification design — so environments support rather than undermine the people working in them.

ND-Accessible Communication Standards

Building communication norms and documentation standards that reduce friction across mixed neurotypes — clear, direct, and structured without being rigid or exclusionary.

Transition & Reentry Support

Practical support for ND individuals navigating transitions across school, work, and life disruption — designed around real constraints, not idealized expectations of how transitions are "supposed" to go.

Neurodivergence as Design Advantage

NeuroFrameWorks™ treats neurodivergence as both a design constraint and a genuine advantage — building environments that fit real humans and unlock what ND minds do exceptionally well, instead of forcing compliance with brittle norms.

How We Work
Environments, Not Individuals

We change workplaces, workflows, and communication norms — not people. ND people are not the problem to be solved. The environments they're asked to operate in often are.

ND-Positive Design

Neurodivergence is not a deficit. Every engagement is built on the premise that good design includes — and benefits from — the full range of how human minds work.

Privacy & Consent-Respecting

No individual is required to disclose a diagnosis to benefit from NeuroFrameWorks™ design. Better systems help everyone — and that is exactly the point.

Non-Clinical Throughout

NeuroFrameWorks™ does not assess, diagnose, or treat. It designs environments and operational structures. Clinical needs belong with qualified professionals.

On neurodivergence and design: When workplaces and workflows are built for the full range of how people actually work — including those with ADHD, autism, dyslexia, and other ND profiles — they tend to work better for everyone. NeuroFrameWorks™ is not an accommodation add-on. It is a design philosophy that produces more functional, more humane environments from the ground up.

What NeuroFrameWorks™ Is Not

These guardrails keep the program firmly grounded in ethical, ND-positive systems design.

✕

Diagnosis, clinical assessment, or medical treatment

✕

A substitute for legal ADA/504 or workplace-compliance counsel

✕

A one-size-fits-all accommodation template

✕

A program that frames neurodivergence as a deficit to be "fixed"

✕

A promise of outcomes without testing, feedback loops, and real constraints

All NeuroFrameWorks™ work must avoid clinical language that implies diagnosis or treatment; respect privacy, consent, and ND self-definition; focus on environmental and structural changes rather than "normalizing" individuals; and document assumptions, adaptations, and limitations transparently.

These guardrails keep the program firmly grounded in ethical, ND-positive systems design — not a compliance checkbox or a deficit-reduction exercise.
