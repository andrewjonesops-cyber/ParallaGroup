CounterCore™ Program

Every decision stands on assumptions. Most of them are never examined.

PrimeAssumptionCore™ makes hidden assumptions visible so leaders know exactly what must be true for a decision to succeed. It identifies implicit premises, maps dependencies, and runs "what breaks if…?" checks to reduce avoidable surprises and overconfident bets. It does not pick the strategy — it clarifies the ground that strategy stands on.

Three Types of Assumptions That Undermine Decisions

Not all hidden assumptions carry the same risk. PrimeAssumptionCore™ distinguishes between three categories that each require different handling.

Type 1
Implicit Premises

Things everyone believes to be true but no one has stated — often because they seem obvious. These are the assumptions most likely to be wrong and least likely to be challenged.

Type 2
Hidden Dependencies

Conditions that must hold for a plan to work — but which are outside the control of the people executing it. When these fail, plans collapse without warning.

Type 3
Ideology-Driven Beliefs

Assumptions that are held because of values, identity, or organizational culture rather than evidence. These are the hardest to surface and the most resistant to revision.

What PrimeAssumptionCore™ Does

By highlighting weak, untested, or ideology-driven assumptions early, PrimeAssumptionCore™ helps teams adjust plans before launch — not after failure.

Assumption Identification & Mapping

Surfacing the implicit premises embedded in a plan or decision — the things everyone is treating as given without having verified they are. Mapping them so their scope, interdependencies, and risk are visible.

Hidden Dependency Exposure

Identifying the external conditions a plan relies on — market behavior, regulatory stability, third-party performance, stakeholder alignment — and clarifying which are assumptions versus verified facts.

Counterfactual Analysis

Running structured "what breaks if this assumption is wrong?" checks against the most critical premises — so the consequences of assumption failure are understood before they occur.

Pre-Decision Challenge Sessions

Structured facilitated sessions that separate signal from noise in complex proposals — giving teams a disciplined opportunity to stress-test their own thinking before resources and reputations are committed.

Assumption Risk Prioritization

Not all assumptions carry equal risk. PrimeAssumptionCore™ prioritizes based on how critical an assumption is to the plan's success and how uncertain or unverified it currently is.

Documented Assumption Register

Every engagement produces a documented record of the assumptions identified, their tested counterfactuals, and their current status — so the foundation of a decision is auditable over time.

On confidence and assumption blindness: The most dangerous decisions are not the ones made with uncertainty — those at least carry appropriate caution. The most dangerous decisions are the ones made with high confidence on unexamined premises. PrimeAssumptionCore™ exists specifically for those moments: when a plan feels solid, momentum is building, and the most important questions have never been asked aloud.

How We Work
Arguments, Not Personalities

Assumption challenges focus on the premises and evidence — not on who proposed the plan or who holds the most authority. The goal is clearer thinking, not political friction.

Politically Neutral

PrimeAssumptionCore™ operates independently of organizational momentum, hierarchy, and preferred outcomes. Its mandate is clarity — not alignment with any particular direction.

Documented & Traceable

Every assumption identified and counterfactual tested is documented — so the analytical work can be reviewed, updated, and referenced as conditions change over time.

Sharpens Decisions, Doesn't Stall Them

Assumption analysis is time-boxed and oriented toward decision readiness — not indefinite deliberation. The output is a stronger decision, not a reason to delay making one.

What PrimeAssumptionCore™ Is Not

These guardrails ensure the program sharpens decisions instead of becoming a power tool, obstruction mechanism, or political forum.

✕

A veto authority over decisions

✕

A strategy or ideation function

✕

A compliance checklist or risk register

✕

An adversarial or political forum

✕

A mechanism to justify inaction or stall decisions indefinitely

All PrimeAssumptionCore™ work must focus on assumptions and evidence rather than personalities; document the assumptions identified and their tested counterfactuals; avoid being used to justify inaction or stall decisions indefinitely; and remain neutral with respect to organizational politics or preferred outcomes.

These guardrails ensure the program operates as a disciplined analytical layer — sharpening decisions rather than becoming an obstacle or a tool for internal power dynamics.

If your organization is about to commit resources, reputation, or people to a plan built on unexamined premises — PrimeAssumptionCore™ is built to surface what needs to be true before you find out the hard way that it isn't.
